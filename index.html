Explore the Ethics and Impacts of Deepfakes

Deepfakes are AI-generated images, videos, or audio that manipulate a person's likeness to make them appear to say or do things they never actually did. In recent years, viral deepfake videos of political leaders and celebrities have spread quickly online, confusing viewers and raising concerns about whether digital content can still be trusted. As this technology becomes more advanced and accessible, it is increasingly used across entertainment, advertising, and social media.

However, deepfakes also pose serious ethical concerns. They are frequently used to spread misinformation, manipulate public opinion, and harm individuals through harassment or defamation. Research shows that deepfakes blur the line between reality and fabrication, making it difficult for users to trust digital content and media sources. This creates broader societal risks, including damage to democratic processes, loss of trust in journalism, and violations of personal identity and consent.
