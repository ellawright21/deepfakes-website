Who is Affected?

Social Media Users

Social media users include a wide range of people who consume and share online content. Many users interact with deepfakes without realizing it. False content may lead to confusion, mistrust, or poor decision-making.

Their interests include:

  • Access to accurate information
  • Tools to identify manipulated content
  • Online safety and trust
  • Protection from misinformation

Long-term exposure to deepfakes may cause users to lose confidence in digital information entirely, making it harder for society to agree on what is true.

Journalists and News Organizations

Journalists and media professionals are responsible for reporting accurate information. Deepfakes make it more difficult to verify sources and maintain public confidence in journalism.

Their interests include:

  • Accuracy and credibility
  • Reliable verification tools
  • Fact-checking efficiency
  • Public trust in reporting

Journalists face pressure to identify manipulated media quickly before misinformation spreads widely online.

Political Candidates and Organizations

Deepfakes are increasingly connected to political misinformation. Manipulated videos or audio clips can spread false narratives about candidates and influence elections.

Their interests include:

  • Protecting public image and credibility
  • Ensuring fair elections
  • Combating misinformation
  • Maintaining public trust

Long-term political misuse of deepfakes could weaken democratic systems by increasing distrust in media and public institutions.

Marketing and Advertising Professionals

Advertising and marketing professionals may use or be affected by deepfake technology in campaigns. Deepfakes could allow companies to create personalized and realistic advertising content, but they also raise concerns about transparency and consumer deception.

Their interests include:

  • Maintaining consumer trust
  • Staying competitive with emerging technologies
  • Creating engaging campaigns
  • Avoiding ethical and legal issues

While marketers may benefit from realistic AI-generated content, consumers and journalists may worry that these same tools make manipulation and deception more difficult to detect.

Individuals Targeted by Deepfakes

People whose likeness is used without consent may face harassment, emotional distress, reputational damage, or defamation.

Their interests include:

  • Privacy and consent
  • Legal protections
  • Control over personal identity
  • Content removal options

For many victims, the emotional and social consequences can continue long after the content is removed.

AI Developers and Technology Companies

These are the companies and developers responsible for creating or improving AI systems capable of generating deepfakes. While many developers use the technology for entertainment, accessibility, or creative purposes, they also face criticism when their tools are misused.

Their interests include:

  • Innovation and technological advancement
  • Freedom of creativity and expression
  • Maintaining public trust in AI
  • Avoiding legal liability and ethical criticism

Developers often face conflicting pressures to encourage innovation while preventing harmful misuse of their technology.
